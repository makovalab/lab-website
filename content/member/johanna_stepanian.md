Johanna has a background in microbiology and computational biology, with a strong interest in the study of evolution. She currently works on the evolution of sex chromosomes across primates. Although skilled in both wet and dry lab work, she is more drawn to the computational side of research. Her long term interests lie in bioinformatics and evolutionary biology, including RNA-seq, genomic diversity, and population structure, driven by a continued interest in bringing computational approaches to biological questions. Outside the lab, she enjoys biking, traveling, and crafts.
